Lobster Risotto finished with mascarpone and aged parmesan

Ingredients

1 liter lobster stock
1 fresh lobster, rough chopped
A few knobs of butter
1 shallot, finely chopped
1 teaspoon garlic, finely chopped
4 oz white wine
6 oz risotto rice par boiled
3 tbsp mascarpone
Salt and Pepper
Lemon Zest

Garnish
Parmesan Flakes
Micro Chervil
Chives

Method

Bring to the boil vegetable nage and add the rice. Return to the boil and cook for 4 minutes until the rice is half-cooked. Drain well in a large sieve, discarding the cooking liquid. Spread the rice out on a large baking tray to cool quickly. (Cover and chill if not serving for a couple of hours.)

Gently poach the lobsters in a court bouillon the stock for 6 minutes, then remove and cool.
Shuck the lobster and cut into medium pieces.

Heat a pan with olive oil and add the shallots and garlic. Sweat them for a few minutes until soft. Saut√© for a minute then pour in the white wine, scraping the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon to deglaze. Boil until almost all reduced then add the lobster stock and rice. Stirring as you go, add more stock as needed.

When the rice is cooked to al dente, add in the mascarpone, lobster, and lemon zest. Stir.

Garnish rice with lobster roe, micro chervil, chives and Parmesan.
